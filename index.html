EV Programme Cashflow Model

Models a 20-year government electric vehicle subsidy programme — projecting station build costs, admin, advertising, and subsidy against foregone petrol tax revenue.

STEP 01

Centralise Assumptions

All rates (discount rate at 7%, inflation, subsidy per EV at £3,000, station capacity) are stored in a single Parameters sheet.

STEP 02

Aggregate Monthly → Yearly

The 'Yearly Car Sales' sheet uses SUMIF to collapse 240 monthly data points into 20 annual figures, applying an escalating EV replacement rate.

SUMIF(year_col, year, sales_col)
STEP 03

Cohort-Style Stock Model

Annual stock of petrol and EV cars is derived through a depletion model — each year's cohort is tracked and retired over time.

STEP 04

Inflation-Adjusted Projections

Station build costs, admin, and advertising are projected using compound inflation, all pulling rates from Parameters.

cost × (1 + inflation)^t
STEP 05

NPV Calculation

A precomputed discount factor column is multiplied against cashflows using SUMPRODUCT for a clean, auditable NPV at 7%.

SUMPRODUCT(cashflows, disc_factors)
STEP 06

Scenario Comparison

The 'Comparisons' sheet consolidates NPV of EV costs vs. lost petrol tax vs. the government budget — all via direct cell references.

Load Annuitant Data

~100 records with identity numbers, dates of birth, retirement dates, current pension amounts, and escalation rates.

06

Cricket Tournament Monte Carlo Simulation

The most unique model — uses Monte Carlo simulation with log-normal distributions to model player performance across 110 players, validated by Chi-squared testing.

Monte Carlo RAND() EXP() Chi-squared SUMIF Audit Trail
Data Flow Architecture
Parameters
Points & auction rules
→
Random Numbers
110×75 RAND() matrix
→
RN Checks
Mean, Var, χ² test
→
Runs / Wickets / Catches
EXP() → ROUND()
→
Averages
Named range pricing
→
Tournament Results
SUMIF league table
STEP 01

Generate Random Matrix

A 110×75 matrix of RAND() values — one row per player, columns for each simulated match and performance metric.

STEP 02

Validate Randomness

Descriptive statistics (mean ≈ 0.5, variance ≈ 1/12) plus a Chi-squared test with Ok/Error flags ensure simulation input quality before any results are accepted.

STEP 03

Log-Normal Performance

RAND() values are passed through EXP() to create log-normal distributions of runs, wickets, and catches, then ROUND()ed to integers.

ROUND(EXP(RAND() × σ + μ))
STEP 04

Auction Pricing

Named ranges (Points_Table, Price_Per_Point, Price_Rounding) make the auction pricing formulas transparent and fully self-documenting.

STEP 05

League Table Aggregation

SUMIF aggregates projected runs, wickets, and catches by team across all simulation runs, producing the final tournament standings.

SUMIF(team_col, team_name, runs_col)
STEP 06

Full Audit Trail

A dedicated Audit Trail sheet documents purpose, data sources, methodology, and limitations — providing complete traceability of every modelling decision.
